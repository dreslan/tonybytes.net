Would you shoot terrorists in a video game? <br/><br/>What about terrorists in real life BUT the player is an armed robot and you can control it using a VR headset?<br/><br/>And what if the terrorists are just people and your government and media and businesses are just pushing a narrative to make money?<br/><br/>Would you still go rampaging?<br/><br/>Dont think you would? Sounds messed up? I don't know, people are more divided than ever before today, and techno fueled propaganda plays a huge role in that.<br/><br/>This book explores that theme, and presents a pretty stark image of a humanity divided and left morally void.<br/><br/>I really enjoyed the story, it made me think about the role technology should have in a large society, and it made me think about the failure modes we must navigate around as a large society attempting to pursue a liberal (in the sense of the renaissance) agenda in order to keep our freedoms (and respect those of others) and, to a larger extent, our collective soul from transfiguring into something monstrous.<br/><br/>Oh, also, the book explores AI, its dangers, blessings, and quandaries. All things we need to think about as much as possible right now.<br/><br/>Please read or listen to this (the narrators are awesome), its short, fun, thought provoking.
